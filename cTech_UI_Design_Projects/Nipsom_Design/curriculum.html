Basic Microbiology, Parasitology, and Entomology

Objectives

  • basic concept of microbiology, parasitology and entomology,
  • basic skills related to microbiology, parasitology and entomology, and
  • develop knowledge of the epidemiology of infectious diseases and the

    • Duration/accreditation

  • Teaching hours: 45 hour (lecture 15 hours + practical 30 hours].
  • Total credit: 2.0 (1.0 credit for lecture +1.0 credit for practical).
  • 1.0 credit = lecture hours 15 = practical hours 30.

    • Major topics:

  • Introduction to medical microbiology.
  • Tuberculosis and Leprosy.
  • Food born microbial illness including salmonellosis, shigellosis.
  • Diseases related with E. coli, Campylobacter and Vibrio cholerae
  • Zoonotic disease including anthrax.
  • Sterilization and disinfections.
  • Tetanus and nosocomial infections.
  • Introduction to immunity and immunizations.
  • Hypersensitivity reactions, auto immune disease and transplantation.
  • Water bacteriology.
  • STDs including AIDS.
  • Fungal diseases.
  • Anti-microbial drug resistance.
  • Viral hepatitis.
  • Rabies
  • Dengue, yellow Fever, Chikungunya.
  • Nipah encephalities and SARS.
  • Influenza, avian influenza and swine Flu.
  • Mumps, measles, rubella.

    • Microbiology practical

  • Handling of microscope.
  • Different method of sterilization and disinfection.
  • Different types of culture media and their use in microbiology
  • aboratory.
  • Gram staining method and microscopic feature of some common
  • gram positive and gram negative organisms.
  • AFB staining method and microscopic identification of mycobacterium tuberculosis and Mycobacterium leprae.
  • Serological test for diagnosis of certain diseases like syphilis, hepatitis B and typhoid fever.

    • Parasitology

  • ntroduction to medical parasitology.
  • Amoebiasis, giardiasis and trichomoniasis.
  • Leishmaniasis (Kala-azar and PKDL).
  • Trypanosomiasis and toxoplasmosis.
  • Intestinal nematode including soil transmitted helminthic infections (STI).
  • Filariasis and others tissue nematodes
  • Tineasis, echinococcosis (hydatid disease) and diphylobothriasis
  • Trematode infections.

    • Practical sessions

  • Introduction to practical approaches in parasitology:
  • Safety measures in the laboratory.
  • Microscope, its use and maintenance.
  • Diagnosis of common intestinal parasitic infestation.
  • Collection, prevention and examination of stool specimen.
  • A Microscopic examination:
    • a. Direct smear b. Saline preparation c. Iodine stained smear
  • Morphology of cysts of Entamoeba histolytica and Giardia intestinalis.
  • Preparation of thick and thin Giemsa stained film for identification of malaria parasite.
  • Diagnosis of kala-azar
  • Morphology of Leishmania spp., (amastigote and promastigote).
  • Sero-diagnosis of Kala-azar using direct agglutination test (DAT) and rk39/immuno chromatographic test (ICT/ELISA).
  • Morphology AL, AD, TT, EV.
  • Microfilaria detection.

    • Entomology and vector control

  • Introduction to medical entomology.
  • Arthropods and arthropod borne diseases.
  • Biology, ecology and control of vectors.
  • Vector borne diseases in Bangladesh: malaria, kala-azar, filariasis, dengue,

    • Chikungunya, enteric diseases.

  • Morphology, life cycle and control of mosquitoes.
  • Morphology, life cycle and control of sand flies, house flies and cockroaches.
  • Morphology, life cycle and control of bed bugs, lice, and fleas.
  • Morphology, life cycle and control of ticks, mites and miscellaneous poisonous,

    • venomous and allergic arthropods.

  • Entomological surveillance: objectives and activities.
  • Insecticides: selection, classification, formulation, resistance, toxicity, safety

    • Practical

  • Demonstration of entomology equipment.
  • Identification of mosquito genera.
  • Identification of sand flies, house flies and cockroaches.
  • Identification of bed bugs, lice and fleas.
  • Identification of hard tick and scabies mites.
  • Demonstration of susceptibility/ resistance and bioassay tests.

MPH (EPID)

Curriculum

  • Each MPH program comprises a total 80 credits equivalent to 1800 (600 ×3) student hours spread over 3 semesters, each having 6 months duration

    • In the 1st semester like all other programs basic public health subjects are taught. Total 15 courses (each course is of 3 credits) are being offered in this semester.

    In the 2nd semester program specific subjects are covered. The second semester of MPH (Epidemiology) program includes 5 courses with 30 credits in total.

  • Course-1 Infectious and Non-communicable Diseases
  • Course-2 Epidemiological Research Methods
  • Course-3 Advanced and Applied Biostatistics and SPSS analysis
  • Course-4 Advanced Epidemiology
  • Course-5 Applied Epidemiology (Field epidemiology and clinical epidemiology)

    • In the 3rd semester thesis work is conducted in one year starting from 2nd semester up to the end of 3rd semester.
