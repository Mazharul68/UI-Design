Training on Radiation Oncology and Physics

by mtiltd | May 15, 2018 | Ministry of Science and Technology, National Training Program

Medical Physics is the application of physics in medicine. It generally applies physics for medical imaging and radiotherapy, although a medical physicist may work in many other areas of healthcare. Biomedical engineering is an interdisciplinary field of advanced knowledge in engineering and science to solve medical and healthcare related problems. The population of Bangladesh is estimated to be about 160 million living on an area of 147,570 square kilometres. The number of cancer patients in the country equals 2,000 per million inhabitants per year. According to WHO, the country needs a total of 160 Centres, 320 LINACS, 640 Medical Physicists and additional diagnostic radiology and nuclear medicine centres. The study of medical physics in Bangladesh is gaining importance day by day as cancer treatment is entering a new era from conventional to conformal therapy technique. The Government of Bangladesh has attached priority to human resource development through training. In this regard, the Project Director of Nuclear Medical Physics Institute, Atomic Energy Research Establishment of Bangladesh organized a local training with the assistance of Management and Training International (MTI) Ltd. on Radiation Oncology and Physics at Nuclear Medical Physics Institute (NMPI), AERE, Ganakbari, Savar, Dhaka. The training was well-managed for 27 Bangladeshi government and non-government officials during 2017-18 financial year.

Learning & Earning Development Project

by mtiltd | Apr 23, 2015 | ICT Division, Ministry of Posts, Telecommunications and Information Technology, National Training Program

Information and Communication Technology Division of the Government of Bangladesh seeks to groom unemployed youths to make them capable of earning foreign exchange. With this objective, the Ministry undertook a countrywide project titled ‘Learning and Earning Development Project’. The main objectives of this program are to develop human resources, generate self-employment and increase national income through computer training. The impact of this training would be development of the IT sector and expansion of e-commerce. Digital Bangladesh implies the broad use of computers, and embodies the modern philosophy of effective and useful utilisation of technology in terms of realizing the promises in education, healthcare, employment generation and poverty reduction. The project underscored a changing attitude, positive thinking and innovative ideas for the success of ‘Digital Bangladesh’ initiatives.

Learning & Earning Development Project

by mtiltd | Apr 23, 2015 | ICT Division, Ministry of Posts, Telecommunications and Information Technology, National Training Program

Information and Communication Technology Division of the Government of Bangladesh seeks to groom unemployed youths to make them capable of earning foreign exchange. With this objective, the Ministry undertook a countrywide project titled ‘Learning and Earning Development Project’. The main objectives of this program are to develop human resources, generate self-employment and increase national income through computer training. The impact of this training would be development of the IT sector and expansion of e-commerce. Digital Bangladesh implies the broad use of computers, and embodies the modern philosophy of effective and useful utilisation of technology in terms of realizing the promises in education, healthcare, employment generation and poverty reduction. The project underscored a changing attitude, positive thinking and innovative ideas for the success of ‘Digital Bangladesh’ initiatives. MTI was awarded the contract to conduct Basic IT/ICT Literacy Training in the district of Sunamganj under Sylhet division. It completed 18 Union level Basic IT/ICT Literacy Training for 360 women, whose educational qualifications were SSC, HSC, or equivalent, from February to August 2015. MTI selected the unions in consultation with the UNOs. Two unions were selected from each of the 9 upazilas. Altogether, MTI covered 18 unions. The participants were selected on the basis of pre-test evaluation. Most of the participants were thrilled and enthusiastic for participating in such an innovative and effective training program.
